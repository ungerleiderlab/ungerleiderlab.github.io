Our lab studies the Epstein Barr Virus, a tumor-associated pathogen that has established a lifelong infection in nearly the entire world's population. EBV promotes the long-term survival and proliferation of host cells and is a causal factor in a number of lymphomas and epithelial cancers. We are interested in how EBV replicates, how infection drives tumorigenesis, and targetable vulnerabilities in EBV-associated cancers.

Software

SpliceTools

A tool to help interpret global splicing alterations (using RNA Sequencing data). SpliceTools can be used to explore similarities between input experimental data and splicing alterations detected following the individual knockdown of nearly 150 RNA binding proteins (raw sequencing data obtained from ENCODE).

Modules:
  • Splice site scoring
  • Intron/Exon sizes
  • Fraction of genes altered
  • Novel (unnanotated) splicing alterations
  • Comparison to RNA binding protein knockdown experiments
  • Predicted neopeptides
  • Nonsense-mediated decay
